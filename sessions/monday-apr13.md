# Monday, April 13 — Day 1 Highlights

## Opening Session

- **412 attendees** at IUG 2026
- **Yoel Goldenberg**, new SVP & GM of Library Software Solutions at Clarivate, addressed the conference. Goldenberg joins from the enterprise AI space — previously CPO at Jacada (conversational AI/automation) and SVP Product Management at Uniphore (AI agents & conversational analytics). ^[1]

## Beacon Award

- Presented by previous IUG Chair **Jeff Campbell**
- **Winner: Rhonda Glazier** — Assistant Dean & Associate Professor at Kraemer Family Library, UCCS. Former IUG Chair who led the organization through its first all-virtual conference in 2021. ^[2]

## Partner Integrations

- **Amazon Business** integration announced
- **Libby / OverDrive** integration announced

## Vega Reports Launch

- **Vega Reports** officially launched, built on a **data lakehouse** architecture
- Rolled out April 7 to all Vega Discover customers
- Pre-built dashboards, custom reports, visitor engagement tracking
- Future integrations planned with Polaris ILS data and OverDrive ^[3] ^[4]

## Key Announcements

- **IUG 2027** will be in **Boston, MA — April 1-3, 2027** ^[5]
- **Clarivate Library Innovation Awards** ceremony (10:45am) recognizing AI-assisted cataloguing, discovery enhancements, and community services ^[6]

## Post-COVID Attendance Trend

| Year | Location | Attendance |
|------|----------|-----------|
| 2024 | Detroit | ~400 |
| 2025 | Denver | 502 |
| 2026 | Chicago | 412 |

Pre-COVID (2019, Phoenix) drew 700+. The virtual conferences (2020-2022) peaked at 1,500+ in 2021 due to low barrier to entry.
